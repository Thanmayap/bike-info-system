    Royal Enfield Hunter 350 Summary

    Price: Royal Enfield Hunter 350 price for its variant - Hunter 350 Standard starts at Rs. 1,37,648. The price for the other variants - Hunter 350 Base Premium, Hunter 350 Mid and Hunter 350 Top are Rs. 1,49,900, Rs. 1,65,139 and Rs. 1,69,810. The mentioned Hunter 350 prices are the average ex-showroom.

    Royal Enfield Hunter 350 is a street bike available in 4 variants and 10 colours. The Royal Enfield Hunter 350 is powered by 349.34cc BS6 engine which develops a power of 20.2 bhp and a torque of 27 Nm. With front disc and rear drum brakes, Royal Enfield Hunter 350 comes up with anti-locking braking system. This Hunter 350 bike weighs 181 kg and has a fuel tank capacity of 13 liters.

    The Royal Enfield Hunter 350 is a neo-retro roadster designed for effortless commuting, powered by a 349cc, air-oil-cooled engine producing 20.2bhp and 27Nm through a five-speed gearbox. It balances accessibility with everyday usability, with a 790mm seat height, 181kg kerb weight, and a 13-litre fuel tank. The digital-analog console, Tripper navigation, slipper clutch, LED lighting, and dual-channel ABS add modern convenience to its classic character.

    Hunter 350 Review for Power and Performance

    4.7
    User Rating (1489)
    • The Hunter 350 is powered by the ever-popular J-Series, 349cc, single-cylinder engine, delivering 20.2bhp, and a healthy 27Nm of torque.
    • Power is transmitted through a five-speed gearbox, which now gets a slipper clutch.
    • The slipper clutch is light and easy to use, making riding at city speeds, or in traffic, a breeze.
    • Keeping in character with its long-stroke nature, the engine has good low-end punch and tractability in higher gears is commendable.
    • City performance and mileage remain the Hunter's strongest assets. Its 13L tank, with a claimed mileage of 35kmpl, is good for a range of 450km between fill-ups.
    • Highway performance is commendable too, with speeds of 100kmph being easy to sustain, with minimal vibrations. The Hunter 350 has a claimed top speed of around 114kmph.

    Hunter 350 Ride Quality and Handling

    4.6
    User Rating (1489)
    • Suspension duties are handled by conventional telescopic forks at the front, with dual shock absorbers at the rear.
    • The front forks come with fork covers as standard, which help in keeping dust away from the fork seals. They also lend a scrambler-esque touch.
    • The rear suspension has been updated, and is now softer and more comfortable over rough patches of road. This can be fine-tuned further, with six levels of preload adjustment.
    • The Hunter 350 has a comfortable riding posture, with midset footpegs.
    • The handlebar is angled slightly towards the rider, giving a more elbows-out, commanding riding stance.

    Hunter 350 Design and Quality

    4.7
    User Rating (1489)
    • The Hunter 350 marks Royal Enfield's first foray into the neo-retro roadster segment.
    • The design sees Royal Enfield's distinctive old-school design language aimed at a younger, urban audience.
    • The teardrop-shaped tank features recesses for the knees, and comes in a seven distinctive colours. The one seen here is the London Red.
    • The Hunter comes in three variants - the Base comes with spoke wheels and tube tyres, while the Mid and Top come with alloy wheels and tubeless tyres.
    • It features black finishing on the engine guards, exhaust, handlebars and trim pieces, with less emphasis on chrome.
    • The rear fascia features a round, compact taillight, flanked by round indicators. It also features well-integrated grab rails, and a saree guard. It doesn't get a tyre hugger, but gets a mudguard extender.
    • On the road, the Hunter has a strong presence, with a design that will appeal to both younger and more mature riders alike.

    Hunter 350 Instrument Cluster

    4.5
    User Rating (1489)
    • The instrument cluster is an analog-digital unit, featuring a digital fuel gauge, low-fuel indicator, eco-riding indicator and neutral light. It also features two trip meters, and distance-to-empty.
    • The instrument cluster can be navigated by means of a dedicated button, located near the clutch lever.
    • Royal Enfield's signature Tripper Navigation module comes standard with the Mid and Top variants, while it can be had as an option with the Base variant.
    • The Tripper module gives access to turn-based navigation and music control, while call alerts/message alerts aren't available. The phone screen also needs to be on continuously, which can affect battery usage.

        Urban Beast with Classic Soul – Hunter 350 Wins Hearts

        2 weeks ago

        Vivek Kumar

        I’ve been riding the Hunter 350 for a few months now, and it’s honestly one of the most enjoyable bikes in its segment. It perfectly blends Royal Enfield’s classic charm with a modern, city-friendly design. The compact size and lighter weight make it super easy to handle in traffic, which is a big plus for daily commuting.
        The engine feels smooth and refined, delivering enough power for both city rides and occasional highway cruising. It’s not built for aggressive speed, but that’s not the point—the ride quality is relaxed and enjoyable. The exhaust note has that signature Royal Enfield thump, which adds to the overall experience.
        The seating is comfortable for short to medium rides, and the upright posture keeps fatigue low. The suspension handles most road conditions decently, though really rough patches can feel a bit stiff.
        What I really love is the styling—it’s minimal, youthful, and stands out without trying too hard. Maintenance and service costs are also reasonable compared to what you’d expect from the brand.
        Overall, the Hunter 350 is a fantastic choice if you want a stylish, reliable, and easy-to-ride motorcycle for everyday use with a touch of classic character.
